# Case Study

## Problem

Service operations planning often collapses uncertainty into a single volume number or hides staffing math inside a spreadsheet. That makes it difficult to discuss risk, challenge assumptions, or explain why a plan needs more coverage.

## Product Decision

QueueCast uses a deliberately transparent trend forecast and exposes every capacity assumption as a control. The chart, weekly table, KPI strip, and staffing brief all derive from the same pure calculation module.

## Workflow

A planner selects a fictional queue, reviews its trend, adjusts demand, agents, productivity, absence, and service buffer, then runs an eight-week simulation. Weekly rows compare the demand range with effective capacity and buffered need. A separate human gate records whether the scenario is adopted.

## Why This Replaces PilotMap

The archived PilotMap demo used generic weighted AI-opportunity scoring, which overlapped other recommendation products. QueueCast adds new evidence: time-series math, uncertainty, scenario simulation, quantitative visualization, capacity planning, and explainable tradeoffs.

## Responsible Scope

The product uses no employee identities or real staffing records. It cannot schedule workers or make employment decisions. The forecast is clearly labeled deterministic and synthetic, and limitations around seasonality, calibration, and labor constraints are documented.

## Evidence

- Mathematical checks for trend, residual uncertainty, capacity, risk, required staffing, invalid assumptions, scoring, and export.
- Browser checks for five scenario controls, Canvas chart pixels, eight weekly rows, a high-risk scenario, human adoption, JSON download, keyboard access, failure recovery, and responsive layout.
- The same workflow is repeated against GitHub Pages before release.

## Result

QueueCast provides a distinct quantitative planning product and completes the five-project standalone foundation with forecasting and uncertainty coverage.
